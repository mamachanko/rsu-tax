How to Get Your Data from Schwab

Find your vesting date & FMV per share

  • Log in to Equity Award Center
  • Navigate to History → Transactions → filter by Vest
  • Each vesting event shows: vest date, shares delivered, and FMV per share on the vest date (USD)
  • This FMV is your USD cost basis per share

Find your sale date & sale price

  • In the same Equity Award Center → History → filter by Sale
  • Each sale shows: sale date, shares sold, and gross proceeds per share (USD)

Download your tax form & export CSV

  • In Schwab: Statements → Account dropdown → select Equity Award Center → enable Tax Forms filter
  • US tax residents receive a 1099-B — reports lot-level sale proceeds, cost basis, and gain/loss (USD)
  • Non-US persons (e.g. German tax residents) typically receive a 1042-S instead — reports US-source income from RSU vesting and any US tax withheld (usually 30%, or a reduced treaty rate)
  • Regardless of which tax form you receive, navigate to Realized Gain/Loss and export the CSV for upload here

1042-S vs 1099-B: The 1042-S covers the withholding on your RSU vesting income, while sale-level data (proceeds, cost basis) comes from the Realized Gain/Loss report. Both US and non-US persons use the same CSV export for this calculator.

ECB EUR/USD exchange rates

This tool fetches ECB reference rates automatically. If you want to verify them manually:

  • Bundesbank → Statistics → Exchange Rates → Euro foreign exchange reference rates
  • ECB Statistical Data Warehouse
  • Look up the USD/EUR rate for each vesting date and each sale date
Note: You cannot use the USD gain/loss figure from Schwab directly for German taxes. German tax law requires converting proceeds and cost basis to EUR separately at the ECB rate for each respective date.
